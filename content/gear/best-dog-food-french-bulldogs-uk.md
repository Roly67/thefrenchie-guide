## What Makes Good French Bulldog Food?

### High-Quality Animal Protein (First Ingredient)

French Bulldogs need quality protein as the first ingredient — not grains, not fillers, not "meat derivatives." Look for named proteins: chicken, salmon, lamb, turkey.

**Common triggers to avoid:**
- Corn, wheat, soy
- Artificial colours and preservatives
- Excessive fillers

### Grain-Free vs. Grain-Inclusive

Some French Bulldogs do better on grain-free diets, particularly those with allergies. Others thrive on quality whole grains like oats and brown rice. Buster does fine with grains. Jago is strictly grain-free.

### Appropriate Calorie Density

French Bulldogs are compact dogs with a tendency toward obesity. Their food needs to be nutrient-dense without being calorie-dense.

## Ingredients to Avoid

- **Meat derivatives/by-products** — vague protein sources
- **Corn and wheat** — common allergens with little nutritional value
- **Artificial preservatives** (BHA, BHT, ethoxyquin)
- **Excessive fat content** — Frenchies gain weight easily
- **Fillers** — bulk without nutrition

## Switching Foods Safely

Never switch dog food suddenly — Frenchie stomachs will rebel. Transition gradually over 7-10 days:

- Days 1-3: 75% old food, 25% new
- Days 4-6: 50/50 mix
- Days 7-9: 25% old, 75% new
- Day 10+: 100% new food

Watch for: loose stools, excessive gas, reluctance to eat, or itching. Any of these may indicate an ingredient doesn't agree with your Frenchie.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much should I feed my French Bulldog?

Most adult Frenchies need 25-30 calories per pound of body weight daily, but this varies by activity level. Buster gets slightly less as he's less active. Jago gets slightly more because he never stops moving.

### Should I feed grain-free?

Only if your Frenchie shows signs of grain sensitivity (itching, digestive issues, ear infections). Consult your vet — the grain-free debate has evolved, and some dogs genuinely do better with quality whole grains.

### Is raw food good for French Bulldogs?

We don't recommend it. Frenchies have sensitive digestive systems, and the risks of bacterial contamination outweigh benefits. Stick to high-quality commercial food formulated for sensitive stomachs.

### Why does my Frenchie fart so much?

Probably the food. Common culprits: grains they're sensitive to, low-quality protein, too many fillers, or eating too quickly (air intake). Try a limited ingredient diet, slow feeder bowl, and higher quality food.
